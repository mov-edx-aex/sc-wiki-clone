"A fleet doctrine is a a set of one or more type of ships with preset fits that are designed to complement each other increasing the overall effectiveness of the whole fleet and its probability of winning engagements or otherwise achieving the objectives of the doctrine. The purpose of the doctrine influences the fleet composition such that each fit is designed to fulfil a specific role while ensuring a uniformity of the engagement profile. Fleet Doctrines that have been tested and proven successful in various battles or trials are adopted as official doctrines for corporations to be used in specific situations. The choice of the doctrine is typically the Fleet Commander's call, while the choice of the ship within the doctrine is typically the fleet members'."
E-Uni - Doctrines
Fleet Ops has several doctrines to choose from to fit a specific task. Whether that task is to storm a C3 and cleanse it of Sleepers, or to roam any space for a Hugs Fleet. On this page we will go over the high level descriptions and provide links to specific doctrines. Some of the doctrines are Alpha friendly, like the A-BC Doctrine (Armor-Battle Cruiser) while others are not, like the Dessie Doctrine (Confessor/Hecate). Temporary links:
  • Armour Doctrine fits
  • Armour Doctrine fits - Logistics
  • Shield Doctrine fits
  • Shield Doctrine fits - Logistics

While the following tables will list T1, T2 and T3 ships, as an alpha you are rarely going to sit in a T2 fitted ship. However, you can spend some extra ISK on Meta modules that will provide similar advantages as the straight T2 modules - at a cost. Always, always, always check with the FC to see if it will be covered by SRP and if so, at what level. SRP is designed for the knowns: mainly the T1 and T2 ships listed below.
